PROJECT CONTEXT

Podcasts are a beautiful thing. They’ve brought radio into the digital age. But I think there’s still lots of room for the medium to evolve. After shooting the breeze with a fellow design student, we realized that sharing a podcast with others is really about sharing a specific moments or idea from that episode. From this insight, we saw the opportunity to rethink the podcast platform. We took 2 weeks to imagine and prototype Readio.

Our Audit

We did a broad scan for common friction-points across podcast platforms including Google Music, Apple’s Podcasts app, and Stitcher.

Discovery Requires Effort

Discovering and subscribing to podcasts requires the intentional action of the user. All platforms place top trending episodes in a separate tab away from the listening experience.

Since 2013, there have been 250,000 unique podcast shows available. Yet listeners subscribe to an average of 6 podcasts - only listening to 5 per week. This told us that there isn’t a huge amount of experimentation occurring.

Categories Are Isolating

Even though podcasts may cover a variety of topics, each gets pigeonholed into only one of several categories, ultimately lowering their discoverability by listeners searching other categories. Category ≠ Subject Matter.

Consider the podcast 99% Invisible by Roman Mars. Episodes can cover business, art, politics, technology, history, sports, and many other topics. Labelling an entire podcasts under a single topic (let alone each episode) seems like an impossible decision to make.

Ideas Are Buried

As mentioned before, it’s a challenge for a user’s social circle to *actually* find the moments or ideas that listeners first cared about, especially when faced with an hour long episode.

Listeners are even pausing episodes and switching to note taking apps like Keep to type out quotes that resonated with them, just so they can be shared later.

Strategy

How might a podcast platform encourage discovery and the collection of ideas?

Podcast transcriptions could inform the podcast listening experience.

Text-based content is far more tangible than audio from both a users and systems perspective. For users, an article format affords key-word searches and call-outs to important ideas. As well, a system that can transcribe every episode means that spoken content can become semantic and linkable data points for learning about both podcasts and their listeners. These were the initial opportunities that led us to prototyping.

Some Key Tenets

Visible

The content of episodes should be visible and tangible for users to access, allowing them to review ideas that stood out while listening.

Connective

In moments when a listener shows an interest in a podcast’s subject matter, they should be connected to other related podcasts.

Familiar

The mental models and interactions already internalized by listeners coming from other platforms should be leveraged to ease adoption.

Readio In Form

Surf Through Trailers

Instead of relying on categories, listeners can swipe through minute-long audio trailers for podcasts. Over time, they’ll be served podcast trailers based on their previous activities such as subscriptions, pins, and listening behaviour.

Learn More

When a trailer sounds interesting, listeners can swipe up to learn more, browse episodes, and begin listening. This gesture lowers the effort to learn about many podcasts quickly.

Scroll. Pin.

Readio plays episodes like familiar podcast apps, but when an idea stands out, it can be read and pinned to the timeline. This screen format combines familiar audio-controls with the affordances of vertical scrolling used by article platforms.

Leaving Screens

84% of listeners play podcasts in the home and 42% play during walks according to Edison Research. We imagined that Readio could go with listeners anywhere and still encourage pinning through inputs like voice and gesture.

Clean Scrubbing

Instead of relying on timecodes to indicate areas of an episode, listeners can scrub through to see pins, comments, and chapter markers - giving an overview of the important moments.

Retrace Moments

Instead of requiring listeners to aimlessly re-listen for ideas they heard, they can search keywords to track them down from within the episode.

Recommendations

When tapping on a sentence to complete actions like pinning, sharing, or copying, Readio suggests podcasts based on keywords from that sentence. From here, listeners can browse and subscribe, encouraging newfound or potential interests of listeners.

Commenting

Readio extends discussions to the audience. Sharing a space to comment on big ideas means listeners can annotate ideas, share thoughts, and expand their understanding of a topic.

Final Thoughts

Looking back, I realize that we found more opportunities at an interaction level then captured by the high-level strategy set early on. Simply having more prototypes led to more productive conversations.

Readio also showed me that it’s important to listen attentively to blue-sky conversations. Sometimes we are talking about future projects without even knowing it.
